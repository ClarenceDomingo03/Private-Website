My Hobbies

Basketball and volleyball are my passions.

Explore My Hobbies

Everyone has something they love to do in their free time, something that makes them feel alive, happy, and free from the stress of daily life. For me, those things are playing basketball and volleyball. These two sports are more than just hobbies—they’re a way for me to stay active, clear my mind, and connect with others.

I’ve always had a deep love for basketball. There’s something about the energy of the game that excites me every time I step onto the court. The sound of the ball bouncing, the swish of the net when a shot goes in, and the fast-paced action make it all so thrilling. When I’m playing basketball, I forget about everything else. It’s just me, the ball, and my teammates, working together to score and defend.

But basketball isn’t the only sport I enjoy. Volleyball is just as important to me. Volleyball has its own kind of beauty. It’s not as fast as basketball, but it’s equally intense in its own way. I love how volleyball brings a different kind of strategy, with each player having to be in sync with one another.

Both basketball and volleyball have taught me valuable life lessons. Through these sports, I’ve learned how to be disciplined and focused. I’ve also learned how to handle pressure, especially when the game is tied, or the points are close. The thrill of competition pushes me to be better every time I play. Sometimes, we win, and other times, we lose. But what matters most is the effort and the lessons we learn from each game.

The best part about these sports is the friendships they’ve helped me build. Whether it’s sharing a high-five after a great play or talking with teammates during breaks, the bond that forms on the court or the field is something special. It’s a feeling of being part of something larger than yourself, of being part of a group that has one goal in mind.

Both basketball and volleyball have become more than just sports to me—they’re an escape, a passion, and a source of joy. Whenever I feel tired or stressed, playing these games helps me clear my mind and refresh my spirit. These hobbies remind me that life isn’t just about the hard work or the pressure we face every day. Sometimes, it’s about enjoying the moment, having fun, and being part of something that makes us feel alive.

About My Favorite Journalists: Atom Araullo & Kara David

Journalism is a noble profession that serves as the voice of truth and a bridge between society and information. Among the many journalists who have inspired countless Filipinos, Atom Araullo and Kara David stand out as my personal favorites. Their dedication to uncovering stories that matter and their commitment to social justice make them remarkable figures in the field of journalism.

Atom Araullo is known for his fearless reporting and in-depth storytelling. He gained recognition for his exceptional coverage of disasters, political issues, and human rights stories. Atom’s ability to connect with people and present stories with empathy and clarity sets him apart. His courage in reporting from the frontlines of natural calamities and war zones shows his unwavering commitment to delivering the truth, no matter the risks. Atom’s work is not just about presenting facts; it is about inspiring people to act and understand the deeper issues affecting society.

On the other hand, Kara David has been a symbol of compassion and advocacy through her thought-provoking documentaries. As a journalist and professor, she has championed the stories of marginalized communities in the Philippines. Her show, I-Witness, has tackled topics such as poverty, education, and health, shedding light on the struggles of ordinary Filipinos. Kara’s approach to journalism is deeply personal, as she immerses herself in the lives of the people she features. This allows her to tell their stories with authenticity and heart, making her work both impactful and inspiring.

As I look up to Atom Araullo and Kara David, I am inspired to be more socially aware and proactive in addressing societal issues. Their work is a testament to the power of storytelling in shaping a better future. They are not just journalists; they are changemakers who use their platform to amplify voices that need to be heard.

Notable Works

ATOM ARAULLO'S KAPIT SA PATALIM: The story sheds light on the resilience and sacrifices of Overseas Filipino Workers (OFWs) who endure separation from their families to provide for a better future. Despite numerous reports of abuse and violence against Filipinos in Kuwait, the country remains the second-highest destination for OFWs, with 100,000 deployments annually. The tragic case of Jeanelyn Villavende, a first-time domestic helper in Kuwait, highlights the harsh realities faced by many Filipino workers abroad. Her death prompted the Department of Labor and Employment to announce a Partial Deployment Ban in Kuwait, disrupting the dreams of aspiring OFWs like Rachel, a mother from Mindanao, who had to leave her children and undergo extensive training and paperwork in hopes of working in Kuwait.

This situation reflects the harsh economic conditions in the Philippines that drive many Filipinos to take significant risks abroad. While the deployment ban aims to ensure safety, it also leaves thousands of hopeful workers, like Rachel, in limbo. This story serves as a wake-up call for the government and society to strengthen protections for OFWs and address the systemic issues of poverty and unemployment. By providing safer pathways and local opportunities, we can help our fellow Filipinos achieve their dreams without compromising their safety and dignity

KARA DAVID'S PAG-ASA SA PAGBASA: The story of Section Darwin at Sauyo High School brings to light a deeply concerning issue in the Philippine education system: students advancing through grade levels despite their inability to read or write. It highlights the struggles of individuals like Louie, a 12-year-old Grade 7 student who works as a scavenger to support his family, and Jack, a 15-year-old who overcame his reading challenges through the encouragement of his teachers and family. These stories illustrate the harsh realities faced by many students, where education competes with poverty, lack of resources, and systemic challenges.

The persistence of students like Jack reflects their determination and potential, but it also raises critical questions about the effectiveness of policies such as the "No Read, No Pass Policy." The blame cannot be pinned on a single entity—it lies in a confluence of factors, including the lack of teacher support, economic hardships, and gaps in parental involvement. Addressing this issue requires collective action from schools, communities, and the government. It calls for stronger enforcement of educational policies, access to remedial programs, and comprehensive support systems to ensure that no child is left behind in their journey toward literacy and learning.
